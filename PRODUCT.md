# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Individuals tracking their own money, and small shared groups (couples, flatmates) splitting expenses. They reach for GoNuts in short bursts — usually on a phone, often right after spending — to log a transaction in seconds, and occasionally settle in for a longer session to review where the month went. They are not accountants; they want clarity and momentum, not spreadsheets.

## Product Purpose

GoNuts is an offline-first PWA for personal and shared expense tracking: income, expenses, investments, recurring transactions, category budgets, life-event "contexts", and group settle-up. It exists to make two things effortless — **entering a transaction fast** and **seeing spending trends clearly** — while working fully offline and syncing when it can. Success = the user logs expenses consistently because logging is frictionless, and trusts the trends because the numbers are clear and honest.

## Brand Personality

Warm, friendly, with a playful edge — a likeable money companion, not a stern banking app. Reassuring and low-anxiety at the moments that matter (balances, over-budget, settle-up), with room for character and small moments of delight ("GoNuts" is allowed to be fun). Voice: plain, human, encouraging; never corporate, never finger-wagging.

## Anti-references

No hard anti-references. The user's overriding constraint: **nothing may slow down entering a number/expense or reading a trend.** Soft guidance derived from the personality: avoid the generic blue-gradient SaaS-dashboard look and the generic AI pastel-tile / cream-default aesthetic, since the brand has its own warm ink/paper/coral identity to express instead.

## Design Principles

1. **Entry is sacred.** The path from intent to a saved transaction is the most important flow in the app; never add a tap, a clipped control, or a hidden field to it.
2. **Trends must be honest and legible.** Numbers, signs, and colors should be unambiguous at a glance; never sacrifice clarity for decoration.
3. **Reassure at the stakes.** Balances, budgets, and settle-up are emotional; use color + words (not color alone) and a calm tone.
4. **One identity, expressed through tokens.** Warmth and personality come from the ink/paper/coral system and typography — applied through design tokens, not one-off hardcoded colors.
5. **Earn the delight.** Playful touches are welcome where they don't cost speed or clarity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 and large/UI text ≥3:1 against its background, full keyboard operability with visible focus, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for every animation, and ≥44×44px touch targets (already enforced globally). Meaning is never conveyed by color alone — pair income/expense/over-budget color with a sign, label, or icon. Known gaps to close to actually meet this bar: bottom-nav inactive label contrast and muted-text-on-paper contrast.
